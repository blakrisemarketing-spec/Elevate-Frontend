# Digital PR & Story Angles

Angles a journalist, newsletter editor, university careers office, or partner would
actually want to feature. Each ties to the founders' real experience or Elevate's
real data. The weekly routine appends and sharpens; a human pitches.

Format:
`- <Angle> — <who'd care> — <the hook> — <proof point>`

## Starter angles (built on what is already true and on-brand)

- **The hidden job market** — career newsletters, university careers offices — most
  roles are filled through relationships before they are advertised, and that
  advantage can be built on purpose with no insider network. Proof: the brand thesis
  and a co-founder landing a fintech role via a cold outreach to the CEO for a job
  that was never posted.
- **Winning a full-ride scholarship from Ghana** — study-abroad publications, student
  communities — what the winning applications actually have in common. Proof:
  Rosemary's University of Manchester full-ride (tuition, accommodation, flights,
  stipend).
- **Rebuilding a career after relocating abroad** — diaspora and immigrant-focused
  outlets — how to translate home-country experience into a UK/US/Canada job search.
  Proof: Naa's relocation, sponsored through a deliberately built relationship.
- **The founder-led alternative to faceless career consultancies** — Ghana business
  and startup press — two practitioners who have been inside hiring and admissions
  decisions, now opening the door for others.

## Rules

- Use only real stories and the canonical stats (6+ years, 96%, 2,000+). Never invent
  a client outcome or a guarantee. Keep the honest caveat on outcomes Elevate does not
  control.
